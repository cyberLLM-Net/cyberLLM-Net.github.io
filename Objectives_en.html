Project Objectives

GO1.- Study the cyber-threat detection based on protocol and network traffic analysis enhanced by LLMs to improve the accuracy and early detection of cyber-threats. Building on the results obtained from our previous project PID2019-1113888GB-I00, where traditional ML and DL techniques were used for this purpose, we propose the use of specific LLMs for networking protocols to facilitate this task and complement the model defined for the SIEM.

GO2.- Study the integration of LLMs used for network traffic analysis with the information from events and rules stored in a SIEM. With the aim of improving accuracy and efficiency in detecting anomalous behaviours or attacks. The vast amount of stored network information and the nature of SIEMs make them suitable candidates for anomaly-based techniques rather than traditional IDS (Intrusion Detection Systems) signature-based approaches.

The specific objectives that will allow the aforementioned general objectives to be achieved are the following:

SO1.- Datasets study and feature extraction for the analysis of network-based threat detection based on LLMs. In this objective we expect to study in detail most datasets publicly available related with cyber-threat detection based on network traffic to define their main characteristics and usability for LLMs training and fine-tuning. To mention a few relevant datasets to be considered: ISCX [Shiravi, 2012], CICDS2017 [Sharafaldin, 2018], Edge-IIoTset [Ferrag, 2022], UMass [Nasr, 2018], MAWI [Borgnat, 2009] or DAD [Vigoya, 2020], a dataset resulted from our previous project PID2019-1113888GB-I00. Also, the use of private data from companies interested in the potential results from this research project may be considered. The aim is to identify and extract a set of common features from different and diverse datasets to be processed by LLM based models.

SO2.- Development of specific LLMs for network-based threat detection. This specific objective aims at creating specific LLMs for networking protocols to identify cyber-threats using network-based features. Two approaches will be defined. Firstly, the use of well-known and pre-trained for text processing LLMs from standard libraries (e.g. Hugging Face) will be considered through the development of transformation and translation techniques from network protocol headers and data to text. Secondly, the design and development from scratch of specific LLMs for network protocols considering the limited scope of protocols headers and fields. Relevant datasets identified in the previous objective, will be used for fine-tuning and training of the proposed LLMs.

SO3.- Analysis and adaptation of the state of the art of integration mechanisms with SIEM using network traffic analysis models. In this specific objective, we will analyse and study the integration mechanisms of events and alerts generated by network traffic analysis models based on LLMs. We will start with an integration using a standard syntax based on Common Event Format (CEF), Log Event Extended Format (LEEF), and YAML or JSON rules format, and adapt or develop new mechanisms as needed.

SO4.- Performance evaluation of LLMs for network-based threat detection and their SIEM integration. This objective is centred in the performance analysis of LLMs developed for network-based threats detection from two perspectives: accuracy and efficiency. Firstly, the LLMs developed will be measured in their ability to improve state-of-the-art detection and early detection of anomalies or attacks in computer networks. Methodology and metrics on early detection from our previous research project PID2019-1113888GB-I00 [López-Vizcaíno, 2022; López-Vizcaíno, 2024], along with the development of new metrics for absolute time aware evaluation will be considered. Secondly, the integration of LLMs in a SIEM involves analysing the effectiveness, efficiency, and overall capabilities of the SIEM once LLMs have been incorporated for network traffic analysis. Specifically, the system's ability to detect new attacks or substantial modifications to known attacks will be examined. The time and resources required for its classification will also be analysed, along with the inference speed that enables real-time responses with low latency. In this specific objective, ethical principles that must be included in this type of development will be taken into account: transparency regarding the openness and clarity in the functioning of AI systems, ensuring that the systems do not reinforce existing bias or inequalities (e.g. gender), and compliance with existing regulations and policies, privacy of personal data and confidential information, responsibility in case of harm or negative consequences of AI, and continuous evaluation of the ethical and social impact, as well as the potential long-term impact.
